CREDITSCORE

2 Words causing mass angst among young people. Don't worry. Hopefully, by the end you will be well informed.

What is Credit Score?

Credit score is a number that reflects the credit worthiness of an individual by taking into account financial history. The better your financial history, the higher your credit rating. As there isn't as universal score, your rating will vary depending on what credit agency you use to calculate it. We have listed a few at the bottom of this page.

Why do i need a good Credit Score?

Having a good credit score proves valuable when borrowing money at low interest rates, applying for mobile phone contracts and when paying for car or home insurance monthly.

DOs

  1. *Apply for credit builder card
    • Use 10-30% of your limit every month & aim to clear all debts within a month.
    • Under section 75 of the Consumer Credit Act, if you purchase something between £100-£30,000 the credit card company can be held equally liable if there is an issue with the item. So, if the company you buy the item from goes out of business, is faulty upon arrival or doesn't arrive at all, your credit card company should get you your money back.
  2. Get on the electroral roll
  3. Prevent opening new bank accounts often
    • When applying for a debit card, the bank does a soft search in your name which stays on your record for 1 year.
    • Credit card applications require hard searches which brings down your credit score and remains onyour record for 1 year.
    • Before applying for a credit card, use an eligibility checker to predict whether you'll be accepted.
  4. Put your name on a bill
    • Gas, electricity, mobile phone etc.
  5. If your purchasing a high value item (e.g. Laptop) and there is an option to pay for the item monthly with 0% interest, consider it. THis shows that you can make monthly payments on time, thus increasing your credit score.
  6. Make sure your credit history is correct and there aren't any mistakes on your file.
* ITS ESSENTIAL YOU HAVE AN INCOME WHEN APPLYING FOR A CREDIT CARD

DONT's

  1. Open bank accounts within 6-12 months of each other.
  2. Don't make multiple credit card applications. Stick to just applying to one. If your application is unsuccessful, try again in 6-12 months.
  3. Don't spend more than 30% of your credit limit in a month.
  4. Don't take too long to pay back your debts. They should be payed back within a month.
